I have an interest in high-performance computing. I was able to explore concurrency programming in my Intro to Computer Systems by building multi-threaded applications designed to complete a task in the shortest time possible. For instance, I worked on a C++ project where I had to escort a rover out of a cave with one billions "rooms" in it, and only one of those rooms had the exit.

The finished program was able to explore around eight million rooms a second, with the rover finding the exit in around two minutes. I was also able to write a program that counted the word frequency of each and every word in the Wikipedia, with the program processing millions of words per second. It was enjoyable being able to make incremental improvements to the algorithms I designed and seeing small inefficiencies being corrected resulting in drastic performance improvements.
